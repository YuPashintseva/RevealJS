Some theory

First of all, Audio API is very powerful ability for controlling audio on the Web.

It allows developers to choose audio sources, create audio visualizations, and even apply spatial effects. Basic audio operations are performed with audio nodes, which are linked together to form an audio routing graph.

Workflow for web audio API:

  • Create audio context
  • Inside the context, create sources â€” such as <audio>
  • Create effects nodes, such as reverb or compressor
  • Choose final destination of audio
  • Connect the sources up to the effects, and the effects to the destination.

Why not just use <audio>?!

If you want make something simple like add audio to the Web and just play and stop it, audio tag is enough. But audio has several limitation that keep it from being reliable for audio-intensive applications. There are some restrinctions related to audio.

Some drowbacks:
  • Limited number of samples can be played at once
  • No real-time effects
  • Create effects nodes, such as reverb or compressor
  • No audio synthesis
  • No way to analyze sound or create visializations
